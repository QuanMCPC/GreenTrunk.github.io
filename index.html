Why can we Not Prove the Speed of Light even though we know it?

The speed of the light which is 299,792,458 m/s has not been proven. This may seem absurd but for centuries the scientists have used a mirror to measure the Two-Way Speed of Light.

This means the light bounces off a plane mirror and then comes back to the source which makes the light cover the same path two times. After which, a device is used to measure the time taken and displays the mean time.

How could this even affect us? Well, suppose we have an astronaut on Mars named Jim and the Space Control Centre on Earth, trying to time their clocks. The Space Control Centre sends a message to Jim, saying,"Hey, it's 6:00 here" which takes 20 mins to reach Mars instead of the 10 minutes conventional time. Jim gets the message at 6:20, but thinking that it took 10 mins for the message to travel to him, sets his clock to 6:10. He, then, sends the message back to earth saying,"Ok, it's 6:10 here".. Now here's the twist, assuming that the light travels at different speeds in different directions, the message instantaneouly reaches Earth at 6:20. The scientists, knowing that it takes 10 mins for the message to reach Earth, think that the clocks have been timed while the clock on Mars lags behind by 10 mins!

It may be possible that light travels at different speeds in different directions because after all, our galaxy is also always moving along with our solar system and different celestial bodies which may drastically affect the speed at which the light moves.
